About Me

Howdy! My name is Christopher Plummer and I’m a Computer Science student from Austin Texas currently enrolled at Texas A&M University also minoring in both Cybersecurity and Game Design. I am a passionate programmer who has been interested in programming from a young age and who is a leader, hardworking, and persistent. I am a person who works well with others, is good at conflict resolving, and has great time management; skills that I believe all programmers should possess. Currently my main interests are in virtual reality, cyberbersecurity, and game development. Let me tell you about my story...

 

At around age 12 my interest in Computer Science was born through the game Minecraft. I played the game for hours on end and became very “addicted”. I eventually became so invested in the game that eventually I wanted to create my own mods for it. To do this, I would attend ID Tech Camps (summer camps devoted to teaching kids computer science concepts) in 2012. My passion for the creative of coding was born there, and I never looked back. From 2012-2016 I would go on to attend 6 total summer camp sessions, not only for Minecraft modding, but for other things like game design in Unreal Engine 4 and level design in TF2 and Portal just to name a few. Later, it was in high school when I decided I wanted to major in Computer Science due to this early interest I had developed for the subject early on. That brings me to today, studying here at Texas A&M University.

 

Extra Info: In my free time I love to mountain bike, write electronic music, and design games. In the music world I am currently writing a lot of synthwave and house music soon to be released on my soundcloud. As for game design I am currently developing an 8v8 capture the flag game in roblox with inspiration from the games Battlefield and Phantom Forces.
