Helpful data visualization:


https://www.cc.gatech.edu/gvu/ii/sportvis/nfldraft/

The visualization shows the history of any given NFL team's draft picks by year for Rounds 1 - 7. It clearly shows the position of the player that was drafted in a given Round by showing the abbreviation of the players position. It also clearly shows the status of any given player (active, on another team, not active, and suspended) using color codes. It is easy to read and they used colors properly which is why I think this is a helpful visualization.

Un-helpful data visualization:


https://www.reddit.com/r/shittydataisbeautiful/comments/fvjhdp/the_yaxis_on_this_graph_of_coronavirus_cases/

This visualization is misleading / not insightful because the Y-Axis does not have a consistent scale. It looks like the scale of the Y-Axis varies between each line in the graph. This skews the look of the graph from its true form and thus is a bad visualization.
